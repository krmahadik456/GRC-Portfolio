## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a comprehensive framework for protecting SecurePay Technologies' information assets from all threats, whether internal or external, deliberate or accidental. This policy demonstrates SecurePay Technologies' commitment to maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information assets and complying with applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- All third-party service providers and business partners
- All information assets, including data, systems, applications, and networks
- All locations where SecurePay Technologies operates
- All devices used to access SecurePay Technologies' information assets, including personal devices (BYOD)

This policy covers the entire lifecycle of information—from creation and storage to transmission and disposal.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Security Governance

SecurePay Technologies shall maintain a formal information security governance structure with clearly defined roles, responsibilities, and accountability for information security.

The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) shall be responsible for the overall security program and shall report directly to the Board of Directors on security matters.

### 3.2 Risk Management

SecurePay Technologies shall implement and maintain a formal risk management program to identify, assess, and treat information security risks.

Risk assessments shall be conducted:
- At least annually
- Whenever significant changes occur in the organization's environment
- When new threats are identified

### 3.3 Asset Management

All information assets shall be inventoried, classified, and assigned an owner.

Assets shall be classified based on their sensitivity and criticality:

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Public** | Information that can be freely shared | Marketing materials, press releases |
| **Internal** | Information for internal use only | Policy documents, meeting minutes |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive information requiring protection | Employee records, financial data |
| **Restricted** | Highly sensitive information requiring highest protection | Customer PII, transaction data, KYC documents |

### 3.4 Access Control

Access to information assets shall be granted based on the principle of "least privilege"—users shall only have access to the resources necessary for their job functions.

Access rights shall be:
- Reviewed and validated quarterly
- Revoked immediately upon termination of employment
- Approved by the data owner

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) shall be required for:
- All remote access
- Access to critical systems
- All administrative accounts

### 3.5 Incident Management

All security incidents shall be reported immediately to the Information Security team.

Security incidents shall be:
- Documented in a formal incident log
- Investigated by the incident response team
- Resolved according to the Incident Response Plan
- Subject to lessons learned review

### 3.6 Business Continuity

SecurePay Technologies shall maintain business continuity and disaster recovery plans to ensure critical operations can continue during and after a security incident.

Business continuity plans shall be:
- Tested at least annually
- Reviewed and updated after significant changes
- Documented and accessible to key personnel

### 3.7 Training and Awareness

All employees shall receive security awareness training:
- Upon hiring
- Annually thereafter
- When significant changes occur in the threat landscape

Security awareness training shall cover:
- Phishing and social engineering
- Password security
- Data protection
- Incident reporting
- Physical security
- Remote working security

### 3.8 Third-Party Security

All third-party vendors shall be assessed for security risks before engagement.

Contracts with third parties shall include:
- Security requirements aligned with this policy
- Right to audit
- Incident reporting obligations
- Data protection requirements

Third-party security posture shall be reviewed annually.

### 3.9 Compliance

SecurePay Technologies shall comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements related to information security.

Compliance shall be:
- Monitored continuously
- Reported to management quarterly
- Subject to internal and external audits

### 3.10 Physical Security

Physical access to facilities housing information assets shall be:
- Controlled through access badges
- Monitored via CCTV
- Subject to visitor management procedures

Visitors shall be:
- Escorted at all times
- Required to sign in and out
- Required to display visitor badges

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Board of Directors** | Ultimate oversight of information security; approves security strategy |
| **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** | Overall security program ownership; reports to the Board |
| **IT Director** | Implementation of security controls; system administration |
| **Security Team** | Security monitoring, incident response, vulnerability management |
| **Data Owners** | Classification and protection of data assets |
| **All Employees** | Comply with security policies; report incidents immediately |
| **HR Department** | Background checks; security awareness training coordination |
| **Legal Department** | Regulatory compliance; contract security review |

---

## 5. Compliance and Enforcement

### 5.1 Compliance Monitoring

Compliance with this policy shall be monitored through:
- Regular internal audits (quarterly)
- Security metrics and reporting
- Incident tracking and analysis
- Employee acknowledgment of policy
- Automated security controls

### 5.2 Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment
- Termination of contracts with third parties
- Legal action where applicable
- Revocation of access privileges

### 5.3 Acknowledgment

All employees shall acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy:
- Upon hiring
- When updates are made to the policy
- Annually during policy review

---

## 6. Review and Update Schedule

This policy shall be reviewed:
- At least annually
- Whenever significant changes occur in:
  - The organization's threat landscape
  - Regulatory requirements
  - Business operations
  - Technology infrastructure

The CISO is responsible for initiating and managing the review process.
